Air here originated over the Gulf of Thailand, clean marine air that read 11.5–15.5
µg/m³ en route, but traveled northwest past Thailand's eastern seaboard industrial
corridor before arriving. The Map Ta Phut and Rayong industrial zone, along with three
coal-fired power plants including BLCP Power, lie 131–133 km upwind and are the most
plausible source of the elevated reading.

The only nearby station, Hua Hin 27 km away, reads 18.8 µg/m³, roughly half this
station's reading. The gap is notable but with only one reference point it is hard to
say whether this station is capturing a localised plume from the industrial cluster or
whether conditions here are genuinely more affected than at Hua Hin. The modelled PM2.5
shows consistently low values in the air arriving here, suggesting the industrial
emissions are too localised for the regional model to fully resolve.

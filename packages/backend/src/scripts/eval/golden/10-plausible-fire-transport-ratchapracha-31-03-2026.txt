Air quality here is Unhealthy for sensitive groups (48.3 µg/m³), with smoke from
agricultural fires burning across central Thailand the dominant cause. The air
originated clean over the Gulf of Thailand at 12.7 µg/m³, but the modelled PM2.5
jumped to 46.4 µg/m³ by the time it reached this area — nearly 1,000 fires detected
along the route over the past three days help explain that rise. Zero rainfall and
humidity below 35% have left nothing to wash the smoke out.

Nearby stations read 21.1 to 51.7 µg/m³, with most in the Moderate range — this
station is on the higher end of the regional picture. Bangkok and Nonthaburi lie
upwind to the south, adding urban background to the arriving smoke.

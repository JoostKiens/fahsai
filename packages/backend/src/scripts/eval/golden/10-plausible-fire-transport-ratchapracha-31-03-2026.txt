Around 1,000 fires have burned across central Thailand along the route this air
traveled, and the modelled PM2.5 tells the story: 12.7 µg/m³ over the Gulf of
Thailand at the origin, still 10.9 µg/m³ as the air crossed the coast, then jumping
to 46.4 µg/m³ by the time it reached central Thailand, the fires loaded the air on
the final leg north. Zero rainfall and low humidity have left no mechanism to wash1
that smoke out.

Nearby stations read 21.1 to 51.7 µg/m³, mostly Moderate, with this station reading
somewhat above the regional average of 31.7 µg/m³, the fires closest to the wind
route appear to be affecting this area more than its immediate neighbours. Bangkok and
Nonthaburi lie upwind to the south, adding urban background pollution to the arriving smoke.

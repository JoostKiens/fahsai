Smoke from agricultural fires burning across central Thailand is the dominant cause here. The air originated clean over the Gulf of Thailand but picked up heavy smoke as it moved north, with nearly 1,000 fires detected along the route over the past few days. Dry conditions kept the smoke from dispersing. No rain fell and humidity dropped as low as 29%.

Bangkok and Nonthaburi lie upwind to the south, adding urban emissions to the arriving smoke. Nearby stations read 21 to 52 µg/m³, with most in the Moderate range. This station is on the higher end of the regional picture.

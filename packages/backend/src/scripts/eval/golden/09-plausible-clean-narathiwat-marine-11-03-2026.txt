Air here arrived from deep in the South China Sea, traveling over 1,600 km of open
water before reaching this station. The modelled PM2.5 was flat at 13.5–14.7 µg/m³
across the entire route, and the reading here at 11.6 µg/m³ is consistent with that
clean maritime origin. White Elephant Park, 61 km away, reads 11.8 µg/m³ — confirming
conditions are similar across the area.

Strong and consistent easterly winds have kept this maritime flow in place for several
days, explaining why conditions here remain clean despite it being peak burning season
across much of mainland Southeast Asia.

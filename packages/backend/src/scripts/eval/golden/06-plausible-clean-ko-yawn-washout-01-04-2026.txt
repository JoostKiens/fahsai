The air reaching this station originated over the Strait of Malacca, where fires
burning in southern Malaysia near the Singapore Strait had loaded it with smoke,
the modelled PM2.5 at the origin was 60.7 µg/m³, well into Unhealthy territory. But
over 76 mm of rain fell along the path as the air traveled north, stripping most of
those particles out before arrival. By the time it reached this station the modelled
value had dropped to 4.8 µg/m³, the rain did most of the work.

The result is a Moderate reading of 21.9 µg/m³ that reflects what the air picked up
locally after the washout rather than the smoke it carried from the south. Nearby
stations range from 11.8 to 38.1 µg/m³, with the spread suggesting local variation
in how much rain fell across the area.

Air arriving here originated over the Strait of Malacca, where fires in southern Malaysia had loaded it with smoke. Over 76 mm of rain fell along the route over the past few days, enough to strip most of those particles out before arrival. The rain did most of the work.

The result is a Moderate reading that reflects what the air picked up locally after the washout, not what it carried from the south. Nearby stations range from 12 to 38 µg/m³, with the spread suggesting local variation in how much rain fell across the area.
